Welcome to Cambodia

Explore the magnificent Angkor Wat, the world's largest religious monument, and other stunning temple complexes that showcase Cambodia's glorious past.

Cambodian Culture

A rich tapestry of traditions, arts, and customs that have evolved over centuries.

The Heart of Khmer Civilization

Cambodian culture has its roots in the 1st to 6th centuries, influenced by India and later by Theravada Buddhism from Sri Lanka. The golden age of the Khmer Empire (9th-15th centuries) produced the magnificent Angkor temples and established cultural traditions that continue today.

Traditional dance, music, and silk weaving are among the many art forms that have been preserved and are still practiced. The famous Apsara dance, depicted in Angkor's bas-reliefs, remains a living tradition.

Travel Tips

Essential information to help you plan your Cambodian adventure.

Visa Requirements

Most visitors need a visa to enter Cambodia. Tourist visas can be obtained on arrival or online through the e-Visa system. Ensure your passport is valid for at least 6 months beyond your planned departure date.

Currency

The official currency is the Cambodian Riel (KHR), but US dollars are widely accepted. Small transactions are often conducted in Riel. ATMs are available in cities and major tourist areas.

Language

Khmer is the official language. English is commonly spoken in tourist areas. Learning a few basic Khmer phrases like "Sous-dey" (Hello) and "Or-kun" (Thank you) will be appreciated.

Best Time to Visit

The cool season (November-February) is ideal with pleasant temperatures. Hot season (March-May) can be very warm. Rainy season (June-October) brings lush landscapes but occasional downpours.

Dress Code

Light, breathable clothing is recommended. When visiting temples, cover shoulders and knees as a sign of respect. Comfortable walking shoes are essential for temple exploration.

Etiquette

Cambodians greet with a "sampeah" (palms together in prayer-like gesture). Avoid touching people's heads or pointing feet at people or sacred objects. Remove shoes when entering homes and some shops.
